構え — deciding before doing

A Kamae document is written before the work starts and read long after it ends. The seed names what the project is and refuses what it is not. The system design turns that refusal into commitments: this stack, this boundary, this test. Nothing downstream reopens them. When a session finds a decision the chain never made, the session stops and the question goes back up. That is the whole discipline — decide where deciding is cheap.

term-dense — marked terms at rest

Every ho (歩) is one bounded step, sized to one session. The Kamae (構え) chain decides before the step; dandori (段取り) orders the work inside it; Reflect records what the step learned. The rhythm underneath is shu-ha-ri: follow the form, adapt the form, leave the form.

Recommendation — A

  1. Body stays sumi; bero stays at the edges. Long-form reading at 14.2:1, with bero-ai carrying only the emergent register and the term marks. B's all-bero prose makes the ink atmospheric — the type is asked to serve reading, not mood — and drops sustained body contrast from 14.2:1 to 5.8:1.
  2. The only marking that is quiet, ≥3:1, and not hue-dependent. A's dotted underline at bero @0.70 measures 3.1:1 as a mark and survives color-blindness because the dot pattern, not the hue, does the marking. C's ink-only term fails color independence; D's sumi @0.40 dots sit at 2.4:1, below the non-text floor.
  3. One foundry, honest pairing. Source Serif 4 and Source Code Pro share proportions, so procedural blocks sit inside prose at 0.875rem with no optical fudging; the mono reads as typed record, not IDE chrome. Plex is the same trick with a colder, more corporate voice; JetBrains Mono shouts editor; Fragment Mono's single weight can't articulate frontmatter without leaning entirely on opacity.

Spectral (C) is genuinely sharp and handsome at 1.125rem, but its light strokes sparkle at caption sizes and its stronger character pulls attention the voice constraint says the type must not take.

Frozen if accepted

Session record — landed 2026-07-04

Winner: A (Source Serif 4 + Source Code Pro), accepted as recommended.
