Session 4 — the step

What one step of motion is — the unit all site motion is built from. Each variant answers with the same three cases: a page transition, a section arriving in-page, and a five-node sequence building piece by piece. Every number printed under a demo is the number driving it.

In every variant the outgoing page is cut, never faded — release is not a step; only arrival commits. Replay resets are instant swaps. Sequence nodes are session 1's solid form (<24px, no keyline, no character).

Recommendation — B, the brisk footfall

1. Only the footfall reads as a step. A step is a bounded move with direction and a landing. B's 8px rise decelerating into rest is exactly that — the eye reads arrived, not appeared or faded in. A (the cut) is honest but mute: at page scale it reads as a glitch, and in a sequence the nodes pop rather than land — presence without commitment. D fails its own test: at 220ms with no travel, opacity alone reads as drift — a position may not wander, and it turns out a surface materializing in place wanders too. The bokashi edge belongs to edges (session 2 already holds it there), not to arrivals.

2. The constant beat is the framework's honest rhythm. Steps are equal commitments; a sequence that accelerates (D) implies momentum, and momentum is drift. B's 300ms constant interval makes a five-node build countable — five equal footfalls, 1.36s total — brisk enough that the reader never waits (C's 2.36s does make you wait, and its 280ms step starts to feel like being shown motion rather than shown work), slow enough that each landing registers.

Secondary: B's page transition (cut out, 80ms washi hold, one 160ms step in) keeps the release instant — leaving is not a step; only arrival commits — and the washi hold is the blank sheet between impressions.

# the step — the unit of all site motion
step duration        160ms
step easing          cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)
                     accelerate → decelerate → land; no overshoot.
                     inside a step only — never between rests.
step travel          8px (0.5rem) vertical rise, translateY(8px → 0)
step opacity         0 → 1, same duration, same curve
hold between steps   140ms
sequence interval    300ms (= step + hold), constant — never accelerates
                     five-node build = 1360ms total

# the three cases
page transition      out: cut, 0ms (release is not a step)
                     hold: 80ms on bare washi
                     in: one step, 160ms — total 240ms
section arrival      one step; the block moves as a single piece
sequence             one step per node, 300ms constant interval

# reduced motion
prefers-reduced-motion: reduce → every step is an instant swap (0ms);
sequences render complete in one frame; holds collapse to 0.

# never moves
- body text mid-read (a section may arrive once, as one block; after
  that its text is ground)
- the washi ground
- anything already at rest — an arrived element never re-travels
- surrounding layout: arrivals occupy reserved space; no reflow shove
- scroll position (no scroll-jacking, ever)
- anything under the pointer: nothing moves beneath a hover
- easing between rests: no element ever glides from one rest to another;
  travel > 8px is a cut plus an arrival, not a longer slide
