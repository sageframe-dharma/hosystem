Ho 7.1 — the vault walker, alone

Ho 7 asked for two things in one session: walk the vault and diff the result. It did not fit. This ho takes the walker alone; the diff moves to ho 7.2.

Think

The walker reads the vault tree once per run and emits a snapshot: every note’s path, mtime, and content hash. Two shapes were on the table — stream entries as the walk finds them, or collect the whole tree and emit once.

Collect-and-emit wins. The diff needs a stable snapshot to compare against; a stream pushes ordering problems into the consumer. The cost is memory proportional to vault size — at eleven thousand notes the snapshot is under four megabytes. Acceptable.

Recommendation — variant D, the plate

  • Faithful without theater. Stacked lines in authored order are the frontmatter as the practitioner typed it. A’s box is chrome the document never had; B’s fences are file-format delimiters, not content — the single rule below the header does the fences’ one job (mark where the header ends) in print terms. Nothing hidden, nothing added.
  • Side-by-side is the true shape of supersession. What fell and what replaced it are parallel states, not a sequence. Stacked (B) reads as revision history; strike-and-replace (C) draws an edit mark through a closed record inside the current document — it brushes against forward-only, which never edits, only supersedes.
  • The chain lives where the data lives. builds-on/supersedes are header facts, so the header carries the links and the state marks. Prose is linked only where the author wrote a link — the site renders what the document does, it does not decorate mentions (the governing test). C’s rail also costs the margin that session 8 (the trail) and session 10 (the split) may need.

Also carried by D: the 12px solid-form koma (session 1’s sub-24px inking) as the state mark on status and every chain target — state is read at the header at a glance, inked per session 2’s map, with labels at full sumi.
