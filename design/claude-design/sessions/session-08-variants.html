Session 8 — the trail

hosystem · one question: how the visitor’s walk renders · frozen inputs read from basis-of-design §1 piece · §2 palette · §3 type · §4 step · §6 diagrams · §7 definition-on-touch (all landed)

The site records the visitor’s path client-side and renders it as a small arc tree: the nav’s planned order beside the walk actually taken, branching where the visitor diverged. It never blocks, nags, or gamifies. It must read in one look or cost nothing to ignore. Gold marks framework state only — visiting is reader state, so the trail is inked in sumi’s opacity ladder, the same grammar §7 landed for met terms.

The simulated visit (all four demos replay it): Practice → Walk (met koma) → Skills (met kamae) → back to Walk → Framework.
Nav order assumed: Framework · Practice · Skills · Writing · Walk · Colophon — placeholder door names from the spec’s content map. Geometry and rules are what this session freezes; names bind at build. A “met” term is one whose pinned definition was dismissed (§7).

Replay compresses the visit: in life each step arrives once, on its own page load, after the 240 ms page transition — one §4 step (160 ms, cubic-bezier(.2,0,0,1), 8 px rise, seat). The demo populates at §4’s 380 ms constant interval. Under prefers-reduced-motion every arrival is an instant swap.

Recommendation — D

D, plan above, walk beneath.

1. The resting image is the argument. The nav’s straight line prints in the faintest ink on the page; the visitor’s zigzag is what gets inked beneath it. The framework’s signature reading — the gap between plan and actual tells the real story — is legible in one look, labeled, with no interaction required. A needs a tap to give up its labels, C needs a page visit, and B shows rhythm but never the gap.

2. Present everywhere, in the way nowhere. The foot of the page is where a reader arrives when they have finished reading — the trail waits there instead of riding the viewport. No fixed overlay, no competition with §7’s card for the page’s only elevated layer, and ignoring it costs exactly nothing.

3. The persistence split matches the grammar. Doors carry the long memory by §2’s opacity ladder (never entered @.40, entered once @.70, entered this walk 1.00); each walk is its own line and dies with the session. The record reads as a footprint, not a dossier — and forward-only is literal: a revisit appends a new node, it never draws a return.

4. Shape discipline holds. §6 reserves the koma for steppable work; page visits are not work, so the trail spends squares and lines, not pieces. B breaks this; D keeps the piece meaning one thing everywhere on the site.
