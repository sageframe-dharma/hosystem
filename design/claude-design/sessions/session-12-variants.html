wayfinding — session 12

One question: how a visitor knows where they are, and moves on from there. Born at the v1 review — the practitioner, who wrote every document on this site, got lost in it: “I need some sort of breadcrumbs”; and, standing on the trail, “why can’t I continue it here.” Three faces, answered together by every variant: a deep page says where it sits (breadcrumbs), the trail offers a way forward (do doors navigate?), and a first look at the strip says what it is (the trail announcing itself).

The trail’s geometry is §8/§11, frozen — every strip below draws it verbatim (right-angle routing per the ledger propagation). This session adds affordance and meaning only. Gold stays state; the crumb and the doors invent no new ink and no new affordance: solid underline = link, dotted = term, everywhere.

each demo is one mock deep page — the real ho-04.6 ledger header under real breadcrumbs — with a simulated 3-step walk: Framework → Practice → Walk · Colophon greets from a past visit (sumi@.70)

everything claimed interactive is interactive: tab reaches crumb and door links, Enter follows · where doors navigate, clicking one appends the step that would arrive with the page (demo route prints beneath the frame in bero — demo chrome, not site ink) · hover/focus/tap a step node for §8 expansion, tap pins, Escape or outside-tap dismisses · replay honors prefers-reduced-motion

Recommendation — A

1. It answers both of the practitioner’s actual reaches, with the least new chrome. “I need some sort of breadcrumbs” gets the path spoken in words at the exact place a lost reader looks — the top of the column — and “why can’t I continue it here” gets the direct answer: now you can; the doors he stood on open. B refuses the second reach entirely (the header nav he was not reaching for is the only way on); D grants both but makes climbing out require the scroll to the foot — a lost reader is lost at the top.

2. Nothing is invented. The crumb reuses §5’s link grammar (solid 1 px bero@.70 underline) and §2’s ink states (current = full ink, ancestors receded to @.70 — the state map’s own reading of “where you stand”); the door affordance is the same underline arriving on the label at touch, so at rest the strip is §8 verbatim and the record never becomes a nav bar. The hint teaches once and disappears — B makes every page carry its lesson forever, which fails terse over a lifetime of visits; C spends chip-and-arrow chrome to say what eleven mono characters say plainly.

3. It survives the site’s real depth mechanically. Short ids in mono make both test cases fit a 360 px column untruncated (“framework / structure / ho-structure” ≈ 289 px at 13 px mono); the overflow rule is one deterministic move (middles collapse right-to-left into a single “…”), not a tuned layout.

If the plain line reads too quiet at judgment, C is the principled alternative — same doors, same inks, the idiom made visible — at the cost of chrome the voice rule would rather not spend. If doors-navigate feels like too much motion toward nav, B is the retreat, and the “continue” question comes back as a new session.
