Session 14 · the header — working demos motion: full (discrete §4 steps)

hosystem · design session 14

The header — does it persist while reading, and if so how it appears, condenses, and leaves

born at the v1 walkthrough (2026-07-07): on long framework pages the practitioner reached twice for persistent navigation. Four working demos below. Prose is sample.

Every header motion here is bound by §4: nothing eases continuously, nothing is scroll-linked. When a header appears or leaves it does so as one committed step — 160 ms, cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1), an 8 px rise with opacity 0→1 — fired by a discrete trigger (a scroll-direction commitment, or a threshold crossed once). It never slides its full height with your finger; a header that tracks the scroll is drift and is disqualified by the grammar, not by taste. Reduced motion removes even the step: the header is simply present or absent.

Two frozen neighbours the header must respect. §8 the trail is a footer strip, in-column, “no fixed elements.” Any header that carries a walk miniature is proposing a §8 propagation — legal only through the ledger, and it says so on the page with the cost. §12 the crumb sits at the very top of the content column (door / collection / record). A docked header and the crumb must read together without redundancy.

A Static baseline — the header prints once at the top and scrolls away with the page; it never returns.
persistence: static (current behavior) · contents at top: full masthead (keyline koma + wordmark + six doors) · walk: absent · boundary: none (nothing docks)
Scroll it. The masthead leaves with the content. Nothing pins.
  • Scroll down: chrome is gone, the whole viewport is reading.
  • To navigate: scroll back to the top — or use the crumb (in-column) for where you stand.
  • The honest argument: the site is quiet; persistent chrome is 44 px of reading spent forever. The crumb (§12) already names where in a door you are; the trail footer (§8) holds the walk. There may be nowhere the reader is that the content column can’t already name.
  • Where it fails: the reach that opened this session — wanting a door mid-read, without leaving your place.
B Always-docked-condensed — a 44 px header is pinned from load, present the whole read, doors inked to state.
persistence: always docked · contents: solid koma h20 + six doors + current-door state (§8 door ink) · walk: absent · boundary: hairline rule 1 px sumi@.12
Scroll it. The header never moves — it is simply always there.
  • The current door prints sumi 1.00, doors you’ve entered sumi@.70, doors you haven’t sumi@.40 (§8 hierarchy, no gold).
  • Body reserves 44 px up top — no reflow, ever; headings carry scroll-margin-top 56 px so anchors clear the bar.
  • Crumb vs header: the header names which door; the crumb names where in the door. The header’s current-door ink and the crumb’s first id echo the same door — a control above, a readout below, not a duplication.
  • Cost: the 44 px is gone for the entire read, whether or not you reach.
C Step-reveal on upward commitment — absent while you read down; it arrives in one §4 step when you reach up, leaves in one when you read on.
persistence: step-reveal (upward commitment) · contents: solid koma h20 + six doors + current-door state · walk: absent · boundary: washi-on-washi shadowless — a 4 px bokashi fold (§2’s only gradient), no rule, no shadow
Scroll down, then flick up. This is the reach that opened the session.
  • Read down: after ~48 px of committed downward scroll the header leaves in one step (8 px lift + fade). Zero chrome while reading.
  • Reach up: ~48 px of committed upward scroll and it arrives in one step. Reaching up is the touch — the doors come with it.
  • Near the top (≤80 px) it is always shown.
  • The edge is a printed fold, not a bar — bokashi sumi@.12→0. It never reads as chrome furniture.
  • Try reduced motion (top toggle): the header just is/ isn’t, 0 ms, same triggers.
D Step-reveal carrying a walk miniature — the header also shows the door you’re in and your step count. This costs a §8 propagation.
persistence: step-reveal (upward commitment) · contents: koma + six doors + state + walk miniature (current door node + n) · boundary: hairline rule 1 px sumi@.12
§8 propagation cost: a walk mark in a fixed header supersedes §8’s “no fixed elements” and splits the walk into two rendering loci — it requires a ledger entry naming the footer strip authoritative and the header mark a derived summary. (A docked bottom rail duplicating the trail is ruled out entirely.)
Scroll down, then flick up. Same reveal as C, plus the walk rides top-right.
  • The miniature: the current door’s 10×10 node (§8 geometry) + a 6×6 filled step node + “n” count.
  • It answers “which door / how far” without scrolling to the footer.
  • But: the walk now renders in two places. If the two ever disagree, which is true? That reconciliation is the propagation’s real weight.

recommendation

Take C — step-reveal on upward commitment, doors + current-door state, washi-on-washi fold. Walk stays in the footer.

The session was born from a reach, and the reach has a direction: up. C answers exactly that gesture and nothing else. It costs zero viewport while reading — the header is simply not there on the way down — and it appears the instant the reader reaches for it. The static baseline (A) is the honest quiet-chrome argument, but the twice-repeated reach is the evidence against it: the reader wanted a door mid-read and had none. B is the safe answer, but it spends 44 px on every reader for the whole read to serve the minority of moments when navigation is wanted. D buys a locator by reopening a just-frozen, elaborately-landed neighbour (§8/§11/§12) and splitting the walk into two loci — too much cost for a convenience the footer already delivers.

Two reasons above the rest:

1. It matches the trigger that generated the session. Reaching up brings the header; reading down dismisses it. The gesture and the behavior are the same motion, so the header is present exactly when reached for and absent exactly when reading — the maximum service at the minimum standing cost.

2. It obeys §4 with no strain and stays in the ink economy. Appear and leave are each one 160 ms step on a discrete direction-commitment — never scroll-linked — and the boundary is a bokashi fold (§2’s one permitted gradient, endpoint sumi@.12), the quietest print-honest edge available, so the header never hardens into a chrome bar. The walk keeps its single frozen home; no ledger entry is spent.

One amendment offered for judgment: C authored with doors and current-door state at reveal (borrowed from B) rather than doors-on-touch — reaching up already is the touch, so a second tap to get the doors would be coy. Orientation ink is free of viewport cost. “Mark-only, doors-on-touch” collapses into this and is not proposed separately.
